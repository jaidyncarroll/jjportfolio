The 28% Website

Building a website for the 28% Women in STEM newsletter

Background

I joined the 28% Newsletter my freshman year of high school, hoping to aid in the newsletter's goal of promoting women in STEM. We created monthly newsletters that included interviews of women in the STEM field, articles about past female scientists, and more!

In 10th grade, I created a website for this club with a team of four people. This website would allow the club to receive more subscribers to the newsletter and to promote their agenda.


The Team

On this project, I worked with three other students in my App Academy class. Our team consisted of two front-end designers and two back-end developers. All of us conducted research in order to better develop a website to meet the client's needs, and came up with an initial design.








My Role

I acted as a designer and front-end developer, coming up with color schemes, choosing fonts, putting together a cohesive layout using HTML and CSS.

Requirements & Needs

Our client wanted a website that would allow people to subscribe to receive the montly newsletter via email. We were also required to show all of past newsletters as well as more information about the club.



The Initial Design

I along with the other primary designer came up with a color scheme for the website as well as fonts to use. Then the team developed an initial prototype on figma.

We designed different versions of the website so that we would have options to choose from. We wanted to create the best user experience possible.

In order to make sure we acheived this, we held focus groups and conducted research to see how our website's design was received by others.

We adjusted the design of our website based off of this research so that we could create the best user experience possible.

Once we updated our design, we conducted one more round of research to get as much feedback as possible. After some more research and updates to our design, we presented our prototype to our client!



The Final Design

For the final design, we kept most of the features from our original design including the color scheme and general layout.

When we built the website itself we had to change the sizing of certain elements and make the layout of the past newsletters page a little more simple. These changes made our design look good and function even better as a website.

The website was built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. When a user subscribes to the newsletter on the website, submitted emails are posted to a google sheets, using a custom API built using the platform SheetDb. The website is deployed through Vercel.
